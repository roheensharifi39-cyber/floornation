# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Dubai and UAE homeowners, interior designers, architects, hospitality teams, and commercial project managers who are evaluating a trusted partner for premium flooring, outdoor structures, decking, and made-to-requirement furniture. Visitors arrive with a property, project brief, inspiration, or material need and want to understand capabilities, see credible work, and start a qualified conversation quickly.

## Product Purpose

Floor Nation's website is a polished sales and portfolio experience that turns broad capabilities into a clear, trustworthy service story. It should demonstrate material expertise and execution quality, make an expanding project portfolio easy to browse, and offer direct lead paths for consultations, quotes, samples, WhatsApp conversations, and custom furniture proposals.

## Brand Personality

Architectural, tactile, assured. The experience should feel established and exacting without becoming cold; premium without theatrical luxury; contemporary to Dubai without relying on predictable regional motifs. The emotional goal is calm confidence in Floor Nation's taste, technical competence, and ability to deliver.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing pages, mass-market ecommerce catalogs, copied Wix or Shopify templates, overlong brochure sites, bright gradients, gratuitous glass effects, card-heavy layouts, oversized rounded containers, cartoon illustration, ornamental luxury clichés, fake awards, and fabricated public-review signals. The site must not imply that custom furniture is fixed inventory or that demo submissions reach a live backend.

## Design Principles

1. Show material judgment through photography, proportion, and concise specification-level copy.
2. Pair premium presentation with obvious next actions at every meaningful decision point.
3. Treat services, projects, and case studies as reusable systems that can grow with the business.
4. Make customization feel guided and consultative rather than transactional.
5. Earn trust through clarity, restraint, real business details, and honest demo-state messaging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA with semantic landmarks, visible keyboard focus, sufficient contrast, descriptive image alternatives, correctly labeled forms and dialogs, comfortable touch targets, and full keyboard operation. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Keep type legible, layouts overflow-free, and lead actions usable from 375px mobile through large desktop displays.
